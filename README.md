Dashboard overview
The SuperStore Sales Analysis Power BI dashboard provides a single‑page view of sales performance across regions, product categories, and cities, allowing business users to monitor revenue, volume, and profitability and quickly spot underperforming areas.
​
​

KPI cards
Total Sales

Displays overall sales amount (2.30M) for the selected period and filters.

Helps users understand the aggregate revenue generated by the SuperStore.
​
​

Average Sales

Shows the average sales value per order (229.86).

Indicates typical order size and supports pricing and discount strategy decisions.
​
​

Number of Items

Reports the total quantity of items sold (10K).

Highlights sales volume and complements the revenue metric to show demand levels.
​
​

Profit Margin

Displays overall profit margin (0.12) across all transactions.

Enables quick assessment of how efficiently sales are converted into profit.
​
​

Filters and layout
Region Slicer

A slicer on the left side that filters the entire dashboard by region (All, West, East, Central, South).

Supports focused regional analysis and comparison between markets.
​
​

Navigation and Page

The dashboard is designed as a single primary report page named “SuperStore,” optimized for high‑level monitoring with the ability to drill into visuals as needed.
​
​

Regional analysis visuals
Total Sales by Region (Line Chart)

X‑axis: Region (West, East, Central, South).

Y‑axis: Total Sales, with a line showing how revenue changes across regions (West highest, South lowest).

Purpose: Compare regional performance and identify areas needing attention or investment.
​
​

Total Sales by Region (Pie Chart)

Slices: Regions with percentage contribution to overall sales.

Purpose: Show each region’s share of total revenue and highlight dominant and weak markets.
​
​

Sum of Sales by Region (Bar Chart)

Horizontal bars representing sales for West, East, Central, and South.

Purpose: Provide an easy‑to‑read ranking of regions by sales volume.
​
​

Total Sales by Region (Right‑Side Bar Chart)

A second regional bar chart aligned with KPI tabs to maintain consistency when users switch between tabs like “Avg Sales,” “Total Sales,” “No of Items,” and “Profit Margin.”

Purpose: Reinforce regional comparison for each selected KPI context.
​
​

Category and product analysis visuals
Number of Items by Category (Donut/ Pie Chart)

Segments: Office Supplies, Furniture, Technology, etc.

Metric: Number of items sold for each category, centered around 10K total items.

Purpose: Understand product mix by volume and identify which categories drive most units sold.
​
​

Total Sales by Product (Bar Chart)

X‑axis: Total Sales; Y‑axis: City or Product entries such as Memphis, Apopka, San Diego, New York, San Francisco, Chicago.

Purpose: Show top‑selling products or cities and support targeting of high‑value locations or items.
​
​

Tabular detail
Sales Summary Table (Country & Category)

Columns: Country (United States), and category columns such as Furniture, Office Supplies, Technology, plus Total sales.

Purpose: Provide a detailed numeric view that complements visuals, useful for export, validation, and ad‑hoc analysis
